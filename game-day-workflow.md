## LIVE GAMEPLAY WORKFLOW

### FOR PROMPT CARDS (5-10 minutes per card)

**STEP 1: Reveal Card** (30 seconds)
- Scroll to the card in your G-doc
- Read it aloud
- Start timer (show on screen if possible)

**STEP 2: Teams Work** (5-10 minutes)
- Teams discuss in breakout rooms
- Monitor time
- Give 1-minute warning

**STEP 3: Submit Prompts** (1 minute)
Choose ONE method:

**Method A - Direct Paste (Recommended):**
- Teams paste directly into G-doc under their team name
- You monitor to ensure no editing other teams' work

**Method B - DM to You:**
- Teams DM you their prompts
- You paste all three into G-doc at once

**STEP 4: Share with Claude** (30 seconds)
In your Claude.ai chat, type:
```
Here are the 3 team prompts for Card S3-01. Please evaluate using the rubric:

Team Red: [paste their prompt]

Team Blue: [paste their prompt]

Team Green: [paste their prompt]
```

**STEP 5: Claude Scores** (2-3 minutes)
- I evaluate all three prompts
- I provide scores + brief justification
- Format will be clear and copy-ready

**STEP 6: Post Scores** (1 minute)
- Copy my response from Claude.ai
- Paste into G-doc under "SCORES:"
- Read scores aloud to class
- Briefly highlight what made top prompt(s) strong

**STEP 7: Update Leaderboard** (30 seconds)
- Update team scores in G-doc
- Optional: Update G-sheets if you have one

**Total Time: ~10-15 minutes per prompt card**

---

### FOR DEBATE CARDS (15-20 minutes per card)

**STEP 1: Reveal Card** (1 minute)
- Scroll to debate card in G-doc
- Read proposition aloud
- Announce prep time: 2 minutes

**STEP 2: Teams Prep** (2 minutes)
- Teams choose FOR or AGAINST
- Prepare arguments in breakout rooms
- Can use AI during prep

**STEP 3: Opening Arguments** (10 minutes)
- Team A presents (5 min max)
- Team B presents (5 min max)
- You use voice-to-text to capture
  - OR: Teams type key points in chat as backup

**STEP 4: Share with Claude** (1 minute)
In Claude.ai chat, type:
```
Here are the debate arguments for Card S3-D1. Please evaluate:

Team Red (FOR):
[paste their argument]

Team Blue (AGAINST):
[paste their argument]
```

**STEP 5: Claude Scores** (2-3 minutes)
- I evaluate both arguments
- I provide scores for each side

**STEP 6: Post Scores & Update** (1 minute)
- Copy scores to G-doc
- Read scores aloud
- Update leaderboard

**Total Time: ~15-20 minutes per debate card**

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**Problem: Teams submit at different times**
- Solution: Set hard deadline, paste all at once when time is up

**Problem: Voice-to-text misses debate points**
- Solution: Ask presenter to repeat or clarify
- Backup: Have team also paste key points in chat

**Problem: Debate goes over time**
- Solution: Give 30-second warning, then cut off politely
- Can allow brief rebuttal if time permits

**Problem: Claude.ai is down or slow**
- Solution: Have backup rubrics printed, score manually
- Or: Postpone card and do different one

**Problem: Students complain about scores**
- Solution: Point to rubric, explain what was missing
- Offer: "You can ask Claude to explain the score more"

## END OF GAME

### Final Leaderboard:
1. Announce winners
2. Highlight best prompt/argument of the day
3. Quick reflection: What did we learn about prompting?

### Debrief Questions:
- What made the highest-scoring prompts effective?
- How is collaborating with AI different from delegating to AI?
- What surprised you about how the AI responded?
- How will you apply this to your own AI use?

### Save Everything:
- Keep G-doc for students to review
- Note which cards worked well (use again)
- Note which cards were confusing (revise or skip)

## QUICK REFERENCE CARD VALUES

**Easy Prompts:** 100-200 points (warm-up/lightning)
**Medium Prompts:** 300 points  
**Hard Prompts:** 400 points
**Debates:** 350-400 points

**Max possible per card varies by rubric:**
- Excellent prompt: 90-100% of card value
- Proficient prompt: 70-89% of card value
- Developing prompt: 50-69% of card value
