## Pre-Game Setup

### Before Students Arrive (15 minutes prep time)

**Materials Checklist:**
- [ ] Card deck printed or ready to display digitally
- [ ] Scoring sheets printed (one per team)
- [ ] Leaderboard visible (Google Sheet projected OR whiteboard)
- [ ] Timer ready (visible to all students)
- [ ] Rubric quick-reference checklists printed
- [ ] Student handouts (17 copies)
- [ ] Team assignments prepared

**Technology Setup:**
- [ ] Projector/screen working
- [ ] WiFi password posted (students need AI access)
- [ ] Leaderboard Google Sheet open (if using digital)
- [ ] Backup: Whiteboard markers if tech fails

**Room Setup:**
- [ ] Three distinct team areas (tables/clusters)
- [ ] Clear view of leaderboard from all team areas
- [ ] Space for teams to consult privately during game

**Team Assignments:**

Base teams on Case 1 & 2 performance to balance skill levels:

**Strategy for Team Assignment:**
1. Identify students who scored highest on Case 1 prompt quality
2. Distribute them across three teams
3. Balance by mixing ages, backgrounds, personalities
4. Avoid putting best friends together (too comfortable = less competitive)
5. Put one "quieter" student per team (first-timer bonus encourages participation)

**Announce teams at start of class** - don't let students self-select.

## How to Run Each Round

### PRE-GAME SETUP (10 minutes - Session 3 only)

**0:00-0:03: Announce Teams**

"Welcome! Today we're playing the AI Challenge Card Game. This is competitive but also collaborativeâ€”you'll learn a ton from watching each other.

I've assigned you to three teams strategically. Here are your teams:

**Red Team:** [Names]  
**Blue Team:** [Names]  
**Yellow Team:** [Names]

Please move to your team areas now. Choose a team captain who will submit your prompts/present debates."

**0:03-0:07: Explain Rules**

"Here's how this works:

1. **Card Types**: Prompt cards (you write prompts for AI) and Debate cards (you argue a position)

2. **Strategic Choice**: When it's your turn, you can ACCEPT the challenge OR PASS. If you pass, you lose 100 points and the next team gets the option.

3. **Points**: Easy prompts = 200 points, Medium = 300, Hard = 400, Debates = 350-400. Your score depends on quality using our rubrics.

4. **First-Timer Bonus**: First time any individual student presents for their team = +100 bonus points. This encourages everyone to participate.

5. **Leaderboard**: You'll see scores update throughout the game on [point to screen/whiteboard].

Questions?"

**0:07-0:10: Distribute Handouts & Show Example**

"Here's your student handout with all the rules and an example card.

Let me show you one example card and how it would be scored..."

[Display an easy card, walk through what makes a good answer using rubric]

### ROUND 1: WARM-UP (20 minutes Session 3, 15 minutes Session 4)

**Purpose**: Practice format, low-stakes, everyone gets comfortable

**Structure**: Rotate through all three teams, one easy card each

**Timing per Card:**
- 5 minutes: Write prompt
- 2 minutes: Score and explain
- 13 seconds: Update leaderboard

**Cards for Session 3 Warm-Up:**
- Card S3-01: Coffee Shop Inventory (Red Team)
- Card S3-02: Social Media Crisis (Blue Team)
- Card S3-03: Restaurant Menu Planning (Yellow Team)

**How to Run:**

**Red Team's Turn:**

1. **Display Card** (or read aloud if printed)
   "Red Team, here's your first card. You have 5 minutes starting... NOW."

2. **Start Timer** (visible to all)
   Students use their devices to write prompt.
   Team discusses and writes together.

3. **At 4 Minutes**: "One minute remaining"

4. **At 5 Minutes**: "Time's up! Red Team, submit your prompt to me."
   [They can email, share doc, or read aloud depending on your preference]

5. **Score Immediately** (2 minutes)
   - Use Prompt Quality Quick-Score Checklist
   - Announce score and brief feedback
   - "Red Team wrote a proficient prompt. They provided good context about Maria's situation and asked for options rather than just an answer. Score: 170 points out of 200. Great start!"

6. **Update Leaderboard** (15 seconds)
   Red Team: 170 points

**Blue Team's Turn** (same process)

**Yellow Team's Turn** (same process)

**After Round 1:**
"Great warm-up! You're getting the hang of it. Scores so far:
- Red Team: 170
- Blue Team: 185
- Yellow Team: 160

Now we move to the main game with higher-stakes cards and strategic decisions. Remember, you can PASS if you don't want a cardâ€”but you lose 100 points."

### ROUND 2: MAIN GAME (75 minutes Session 3, 90 minutes Session 4)

**Purpose**: Competitive gameplay, strategic decisions, mix of prompts and debates

**Structure**: 
- Mix of Medium/Hard Prompts and Debates
- Teams can ACCEPT or PASS
- Includes AI Consultation Breaks

**Cards for Session 3 Main Game:**
- 6 Medium Prompts (300 pts each)
- 3 Hard Prompts (400 pts each)
- 4 Debates (350 pts each)

**Turn Order**: Rotate (Red â†’ Blue â†’ Yellow â†’ Red...)

**Timing per Prompt Card:**
- Team decides: Accept or Pass (30 seconds)
- If ACCEPT: 5 minutes to write, 2 minutes to score
- If PASS: Next team gets option (30 seconds each until accepted)

**Timing per Debate Card:**
- Team decides: Accept or Pass (30 seconds)
- If ACCEPT: Choose FOR/AGAINST (30 seconds)
- Prep time: 2 minutes (can use AI)
- Presentation: 5 minutes
- Opposing teams vote: 1 minute
- Score: 2 minutes

**How to Handle Turn:**

**Display Card (30 seconds):**
"Red Team, this is a Medium Prompt Card worth 300 points. You have 30 seconds to decide: ACCEPT or PASS?"

**If ACCEPT:**
"Red Team accepts! You have 5 minutes starting NOW."
[Timer starts, proceed as normal]

**If PASS:**
"Red Team passes. Minus 100 points from Red Team.
Blue Team, you now have the option to ACCEPT or PASS this card?"

[Continue until a team accepts or all teams pass]

**If ALL Teams Pass:**
"All teams passed. This card is discarded. No points awarded or lost beyond the pass penalties. Moving to next card."

**AI Consultation Breaks** (Schedule 2-3 per session):**

After every 4-5 cards:
"Consultation break! You have 5 minutes to use AI tools to research, strategize, or prepare for upcoming cards. Teams, huddle up and decide how to use this time wisely."

[This gives teams breathing room and encourages AI use]

**Managing Time:**

If running behind:
- Shorten consultation breaks to 3 minutes
- Speed up scoring ("I'll announce scores, full feedback posted later")
- Skip one debate card if necessary

If running ahead:
- Add an extra debate card
- Longer consultation breaks
- More detailed feedback during scoring

### ROUND 3: LIGHTNING ROUND (15 minutes both sessions)

**Purpose**: Catch-up opportunity for losing team, high-energy finale

**Structure**: Rapid-fire easy prompts, 90 seconds each

**Cards**: 2-3 easy prompts from lightning round section

**Special Rule**: Losing team (lowest score) gets first pick on every card in lightning round

**How to Run:**

"We're entering the Lightning Round! These are fastâ€”90 seconds to write your prompt.

Special rule: The team in last place gets first choice on every card. If they pass, second-place team gets next option, etc.

Current standings:
- Blue Team: 2,250 points (1st)
- Red Team: 2,180 points (2nd)
- Yellow Team: 1,840 points (3rd)

Yellow Team, you get first choice on every lightning card!"

**Lightning Card Process:**

1. Display card
2. Losing team: "ACCEPT or PASS?" (10 seconds)
3. If ACCEPT: 90 seconds to write, 1 minute to score
4. If PASS: Next team gets option

**Generous Scoring in Lightning:**
- 200 points if reasonable
- 150 points if weak but shows effort  
- 100 points for participation

**Purpose is high-energy finale**, not rigorous evaluation.

## Scoring Quickly

**Use the Quick-Score Checklist:**

Print this and keep next to you during game:

```

â–¡ Provides clear business context
â–¡ Explains "why" they're asking
â–¡ Includes relevant constraints

â–¡ Asks AI to help think (not decide)
â–¡ Goes beyond just getting information
â–¡ Shows business judgment

â–¡ Plans to evaluate AI's response
â–¡ Shows skepticism/critical thinking
â–¡ Not just accepting first answer

â–¡ Has follow-up questions planned
â–¡ Shows rounds of refinement
â–¡ Building toward deeper understanding

â€¢ 90-100% = Exceptional
â€¢ 80-89% = Proficient  
â€¢ 70-79% = Developing
â€¢ 60-69% = Needs Improvement
â€¢ Below 60% = Insufficient
```

**Fast Scoring Process:**

1. **Skim the prompt** (20 seconds)
   Get general sense of quality

2. **Check boxes** (30 seconds)
   Quickly identify which criteria met

3. **Assign percentage** (10 seconds)
   Based on boxes checked

4. **Calculate points** (10 seconds)
   Percentage Ã— Card Value

5. **Announce with brief feedback** (50 seconds)
   "Blue Team scored 85% - proficient. Good context provided, strong strategic thinking. Minor: Could have stronger critical evaluation plan. 255 points."

**Total Time: 2 minutes**

### Debate Card Scoring (Target: 2 minutes per debate)

**Use the Debate Quick-Score Checklist:**

```
DEBATE SCORING CHECKLIST

Position Clarity (50 pts)
â–¡ Clearly stated FOR or AGAINST = 50
â–¡ Somewhat clear = 40
â–¡ Unclear = 30

Evidence/Examples (75 pts)
â–¡ Specific examples, data, cases = 75
â–¡ General examples, scenarios = 60
â–¡ Vague or no examples = 45

â–¡ Strong opposing view addressed fairly = 75
â–¡ Mentioned opposing view = 60
â–¡ Ignored or strawman = 45

Logic & Reasoning (75 pts)
â–¡ Clear logical progression, sound = 75
â–¡ Generally logical, minor gaps = 60
â–¡ Flawed logic or emotional only = 45

BASE SCORE: _____ / 275

Peer Vote (75 pts)
Did opposing team(s) vote this team won?
â–¡ YES from at least one team = +75
â–¡ NO from all teams = +0

```

**Fast Scoring Process:**

1. **Check boxes during presentation** (real-time)
   Don't wait until end

2. **Calculate base score** (20 seconds)
   Add up the checked box values

3. **Conduct peer vote** (1 minute)
   "Red and Yellow Teams, did Blue Team win this debate?"
   [Each team confers 20 seconds, votes]

4. **Add peer vote bonus** (10 seconds)

5. **Announce score** (30 seconds)
   "Blue Team: Position was clear (50), strong examples (75), addressed counterargument (75), solid logic (75) = 275 base. Red Team voted they won (+75). Final: 350 points!"

**Total Time: 2 minutes**

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Issue: Team is stuck/confused on a card**

**Solution:** 
- At 2-minute mark, offer hint: "Think about who's affected by this decision."
- Don't give answer, just help them think
- If really stuck at 4 minutes: "What would you ask your manager if you were in this situation?"

**Issue: One student dominating their team**

**Solution:**
- Remind about first-timer bonus: "Remember, each NEW person who presents gets your team +100 points."
- During consultation break, quietly tell dominator: "You're doing greatâ€”now help your teammates step up. The bonus points reward spreading the work."

**Issue: Debate is going over 5 minutes**

**Solution:**
- At 5 minutes: "Wrap up your final point in the next 20 seconds."
- Don't be rigid, but keep it moving
- If they keep going at 6 minutes: "Thank you, that's time. Let's move to voting."

**Issue: All teams pass on a card**

**Solution:**
- "No problem! This card is discarded. Moving on."
- Don't make a big deal of it
- Note for next semester: that card might be too hard or confusing

**Issue: Debate peer vote is tied or confusing**

**Solution:**
- If both opposing teams vote NO: Team gets base score only (no +75 bonus)
- If one votes YES and one votes NO: Team gets the +75 bonus (benefit of doubt)
- If teams are confused about how to vote: "Did their argument convince you? If yes, vote yes."

**Issue: Technology fails (projector dies, WiFi out)**

**Solution:**
- Backup: Use printed cards and whiteboard
- Backup: Hotspot from your phone for WiFi
- Worst case: Run game verbally (read cards aloud, track scores on paper)

**Issue: Running significantly over time**

**Solution:**
- Cut consultation breaks to 2 minutes
- Lightning round: Only 1 card instead of 2-3
- Skip final debate card in main game
- Shorten debrief to 5 minutes

**Issue: Running significantly under time**

**Solution:**
- Add extra consultation break
- Add bonus debate card
- Lightning round: Do 4 cards instead of 2
- Longer debrief discussion

**Issue: Scoring disputes**

**Solution:**
- Student questions their score: "I used the rubric consistently. Let me show you where your prompt scored well and where it could improve."
- If they used a Power-Up (Challenge Score card): Re-evaluate more carefully
- Final call is yours, but be fair and consistent

**Issue: Team chemistry problems**

**Solution:**
- If team members aren't collaborating: During consultation break, check in privately
- Remind them it's TEAM score (they succeed together)
- For Session 4, consider minor team adjustments if serious issues

## Debrief Questions

### Essential Questions (Choose 2-3 per Session)

**About Prompt Quality:**

1. "What made the highest-scoring prompts effective? What patterns did you notice?"
   
   *Listen for:* Students articulating specificity, strategic thinking, critical evaluation, iteration

2. "Think about your first prompt vs. your last prompt today. How did your approach evolve?"
   
   *Listen for:* Learning from feedback, learning from others, getting more strategic

3. "What was hardest about writing good prompts under time pressure?"
   
   *Listen for:* Balancing context vs. time, strategic thinking vs. just asking, iteration challenges

**About AI Collaboration:**

4. "How did you use AI tools during the consultation breaks? What worked?"
   
   *Listen for:* Research strategies, how they evaluated AI responses, team coordination

5. "When did AI help you most? When did it not help?"
   
   *Listen for:* Awareness of AI strengths/limitations, critical evaluation

6. "How is using AI in this game different from how you use it at work or school?"
   
   *Listen for:* Time pressure, strategic thinking, collaboration dynamics

**About Debates:**

7. "Which debate argument surprised you most or changed your thinking?"
   
   *Listen for:* Intellectual humility, openness to other perspectives

8. "What made the winning debate arguments compelling?"
   
   *Listen for:* Evidence, addressing counterarguments, logical structure, examples

9. "Did anyone change their mind about any AI ethics topic today? What argument convinced you?"
   
   *Listen for:* Genuine engagement with complexity, peer influence

**About Strategy:**

10. "Looking at the final scores, what strategic choices mattered most?"
    
    *Listen for:* When to pass vs. accept, first-timer bonus strategy, risk/reward thinking

11. "What would you do differently if we played again?"
    
    *Listen for:* Metacognition, learning from experience

12. "How did you decide whether to accept or pass on cards?"
    
    *Listen for:* Risk assessment, confidence calibration, team coordination

**About Real-World Application:**

13. "How will you use these prompting skills in your actual work?"
    
    *Listen for:* Concrete examples, transfer to job situations

14. "Which scenario today felt most relevant to your career or industry?"
    
    *Listen for:* Personal connections, real-world relevance

15. "What did you learn from watching other teams' approaches?"
    
    *Listen for:* Peer learning, multiple valid approaches, learning from mistakes/successes

### Optional Follow-Up Questions

**If conversation goes well and time allows:**

- "What surprised you about AI's capabilities or limitations today?"
- "How did competition affect your learning? Was it helpful or distracting?"
- "What would make this game better for next semester's students?"
- "Did the first-timer bonus achieve its goal? Did it encourage participation?"

## Tips for Session 4

### Session 4 Is Different

**Assumptions You Can Make:**
- Students know the format (faster setup)
- Teams have chemistry established
- Students understand scoring system
- Prompts can be more sophisticated

**Adjustments for Session 4:**

1. **Shorter Warm-Up** (15 min instead of 20)
   - Students don't need as much practice
   - Get to main game faster

2. **Harder Cards**
   - More Hard Prompts (6 instead of 3)
   - More Debates (5 instead of 4)
   - Higher expectations

3. **Championship Atmosphere**
   - Build energy: "This is your championship round!"
   - Maybe small prize for winning team? (Bragging rights? Extra credit? Candy?)

4. **Deeper Debrief**
   - Compare Session 3 vs Session 4 performance
   - Reflect on growth over two sessions

## Grading After Game

### Individual Participation Grades (20% of Sessions 3-4 Grade)

**Based on:**
- Did student contribute prompts/debates?
- Did student earn first-timer bonus?
- Quality of individual contributions
- Teamwork and engagement

**Quick Rubric:**

**Excellent (95-100%):**
- Presented at least once (earned first-timer bonus)
- High-quality contributions
- Strong teamwork
- Fully engaged throughout

**Good (85-94%):**
- Participated actively even if didn't present
- Solid contributions
- Good teamwork
- Engaged most of the time

**Proficient (75-84%):**
- Some participation
- Adequate contributions
- Teamwork acceptable
- Mostly engaged

**Needs Improvement (Below 75%):**
- Minimal participation
- Weak contributions
- Poor teamwork or disengaged

**Track This During Game:**
Keep notes on who presents, quality of contributions, teamwork issues.

## Emergency Backup Plan

### If Game Completely Fails

**Reasons game might fail:**
- Technology complete breakdown
- Students extremely confused despite explanations
- Time mismanagement catastrophe
- Unexpected circumstances (fire drill, etc.)

**Backup Activity:**

**Mini Case Study** (if >30 minutes remain):
- Use one of the Hard Prompt cards as a mini case study
- Have students individually write prompts
- Discuss as whole class
- Grade as regular assignment

**Structured Discussion** (if <30 minutes remain):
- Use debate cards as discussion topics
- Whole-class debate format
- Less structured, but still valuable

**Don't Panic:**
- Students will still learn even if game doesn't go perfectly
- Be flexible and adaptive
- Can always run Session 4 as originally planned
